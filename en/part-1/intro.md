# Part I  Role and Mindset

The first week into an FDE job, the biggest source of confusion is rarely "I don't know the tech." It's not knowing where your time should be going.

Meeting with the customer, writing a demo, labeling an Eval set, pulling Ops into a Slack channel to wire up SSO — every one of these looks like real work, and none of them looks like "engineering" in the traditional sense. Part I doesn't teach any specific technology. Its only job, before you open the IDE, is to set up a coordinate system in your head: what an FDE actually does in a week, how to tell right from wrong, and which class of situation you're currently in.

The other six Parts all assume you already have this coordinate system.

---

Chapter 1 walks you through the shape of a single FDE day, then through the four phases a project goes through from kickoff to steady state (Discovery / Scaffolding / Production / Handoff). After you read it, you should be able to look at any slot on your calendar and immediately locate "what phase am I in, does this thing count as real work."

Chapter 2 covers the three iron rules — Sell Outcomes, Fix Forward, Eval-driven. These aren't slogans. They're judgment yardsticks ground out by repeated friction in customer conference rooms — new requests landing mid-meeting, cross-time-zone outages, CTOs wanting a decision now. Whichever rule you fail to hold, the engineering work in that phase deforms. Everything specific in Parts II–VII derives from these three.

Chapter 3 covers the two FDE modes — data-driven (closer to Palantir-style field delivery) and LLM-driven (closer to OpenAI / Anthropic-style application work). In real customer engagements both modes often coexist inside the same project, just with different weights. This chapter helps you judge which side to lean on for the customer and the phase you're currently in.

---

After reading Part I, you won't have written a single line of code, but you should be able to make three judgments more confidently: how to allocate today's time, what phase your current project is in, and which class of FDE work you're facing.

The remaining six Parts all build on top of those three judgments.
